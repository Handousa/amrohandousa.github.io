About Me

I am an entrepreneur who breathes science and speaks logic with great interest in business analytics and machine learning applications. I came to the United States in August 2012 inspired by Steve Jobs and Apple’s campaign “Think Different.”

Data Analytics... How it all started?

My passion for data analytics and machine learning grew in me when I started my master's research in renewable energy systems design and energy management applications. After spending about two years in research, I realized the importance of data collection and delivering business insights from this data for justifying project funding and resource allocation. This is why I decided to pursue a Ph.D. degree in computer science focusing on machine learning applications. As an entrepreneur, I thrive to find business applications from current academic research. I am currently working with an NLP research group in feature extraction and sentiment analysis by applying BERT which is Google’s latest technology. I am also developing a social media platform that will be available in the market before the end of this year which will benefit f rom my NLP research and will have several machine learning potential applications.

I enjoy working with STUDENTS

I like to mention also that I am very passionate about working in the academic fields and research institutions. It all started when I applied to become a tutor and later on a Supplemental Instruction (SI) Leader in the Tutoring and Academic Success Center (TASC) at Cleveland State University. Spending over four years between tutoring and teaching assistance at Cleveland State University, I have found that my main objective is to open a new world of opportunities and help students realize their full potential. Everyone is special and I enjoy helping them see it. Moreover, through my experience, I saw the importance of giving the students the necessary skills to become independent learners, excellent time managers as these are essential for success in their programs.

During Fall 2020 as the Teaching Assistant for Introduction to Robotics, in the lab I have managed to make sure that the entire class is moving forward as one entity while giving personal attention to every student since they come from different backgrounds and learning skills. I do this by applying diverse methods of instruction to ensure that the course objectives are met, and the students' expectations are exceeded. The two most common strategies I incorporate in the sessions are case studies and collaborative learning. Case studies are very important for the students as it helps them find values in what they are learning to ensures the students that this class is worth their money, time, and most importantly their attention. While collaborative activities are limit now due to COVID-19, it helps the students to grasp a holistic overview of the material, makes them active, and encourages participation.
